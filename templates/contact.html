FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the most common questions about KidneyHealth AI.

What types of kidney conditions can the system detect?

The system classifies CT scan images into four categories: Normal (healthy kidney), Cyst (fluid-filled sac), Stone (mineral deposits), and Tumor (abnormal tissue growth). Each prediction comes with a confidence score.

How accurate is the AI model?

Our VGG16-based model achieves over 95% validation accuracy on our curated dataset. Accuracy may vary on clinical images with varying contrast, resolution, or imaging protocols. Always validate results with a radiologist.

Is my CT scan data stored anywhere?

No. Images are decoded server-side for inference and immediately discarded. We do not store, log, or share any uploaded image data. Processing is ephemeral and privacy-aligned.

Can I use this as a final medical diagnosis?

No. This tool is designed as a research-grade assistive tool only. It should never replace the judgment of a qualified nephrologist, radiologist, or urologist. Always seek professional medical advice.

What image format should I upload?

The system accepts JPG, JPEG, and PNG images. Axial-view CT scan images produce the best results. Images are automatically resized to 224×224 pixels for model inference. Max file size is 10MB.

How do I integrate this into our hospital system?

KidneyHealth AI exposes a REST API (POST /predict) that accepts base64-encoded images and returns JSON predictions. Contact us at info@kidneyhealthai.com to discuss integration, API documentation, and institutional licensing.
